## 2. WHO SAGO — Independent Assessment (27 June 2025)

WHO Scientific Advisory Group for the Origins of Novel Pathogens (27 experts):

- "All hypotheses remain on the table" including laboratory-associated exposure
- Origins remain unresolved "because of missing data — particularly from China"
- Multiple hypotheses: direct bat spillover, intermediate host, AND lab-associated exposure
- Early patient sequences remain absent from public databases

**Sources:**
- who.int/news/item/27-06-2025-who-scientific-advisory-group-issues-report-on-origins-of-covid-19
- cdn.who.int/media/docs/default-source/documents/epp/sago/independent-assessment-of-the-origins-of-sars-cov-2-by-sago.pdf
- bmj.com/content/389/bmj.r1358 — "All hypotheses remain viable"

**Confidence: HIGH** (primary: WHO official publication)

## 4. Lifebit Mirror — Collection Dates Confirmed

## 5. House COVID Panel Report (Dec 2024)

The Republican-led House Select Subcommittee on the Coronavirus Pandemic released a 520-page final report on 2 December 2024:

- Concluded virus "most likely emerged from a laboratory in Wuhan, China"
- Cited biological characteristics of the virus and illnesses among researchers
- No new direct evidence of a lab leak; circumstantial case
- WIV used NIAID money for "gain-of-function" studies on distantly related coronaviruses

**Source:** oversight.house.gov/release/final-report-covid-select-concludes-2-year-investigation/ — science.org/content/article/house-panel-concludes-covid-19-pandemic-came-lab-leak

**Confidence: HIGH** (primary: House of Representatives official report)
